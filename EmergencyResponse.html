Emergency Response Procedure

OBJECTIVES OF THE EMERGENCY RESPONSE PROCEDURE-

  1. To control, localize and eliminate the hazards in minimum time.
  2. To minimize damage to property and environment.
  3. To render medical treatment to the injured quickly
  4. To speed up the rescue and head count operation
  5. To safe guard others by timely evacuation
  6. To ensure safety of the installation and persons before they re-enter and resume the work.
  7. To restore normalcy as quickly as possible.

AN EMERGENCY SITUATION CAN ARISE DUE TO-

  1. Fire
  2. Earthquake
  3. Active shooter
  4. CPR Patient
  5. Harassment

(i) Fire-

Know the location and operation of fire extinguishers, fire exits, and pull stations alarms in your area.

In the event of a fire 'RACE'

R - Rescue any victims in immediate danger.
A - Alarm, turn in the alarm, active the pull station alarm to notify Campus Police.
C - Confine the smoke and fire to the area of origin by closing the door.
E - Extinguish -or- Evacuate use a fire extinguisher to put out the fire if it is small enough for you to safely do so. If you are unsure or unable to do this then evacuate the immediate area.

Fire Extinguisher Use
Extinguishers are located throughout all campus buildings. Identify the two extinguishers nearest the areas where you work, teach, attend class, or spend time.

P=Pull the pin
A= Aim at the base of the fire.
S=Squeeze the handle.
S=Sweep side to side.

(ii) Active Shooter-

Run, Hide, Fight

As a standard response to active shooter/armed aggressor/active violence incidents, COM uses the Run/Hide/Fight response.

Run: Escape if possible
Hide: If escape is not possible. Barricade doors. Hide behind heavy furniture.
Fight: Only as a last resort. Use objects as weapons. Work with others.

(iii) Earthquake-

(a) During Earthquake-

1.Stay calm and try to calm and reassure others.

2.If indoors, stay indoors. Seek shelter under a sturdy desk or table. Cover your head and torso to protect yourself from objects that could fall during an earthquake. Make every attempt to hold on to the piece of furniture in order to remain protected (large earthquakes will cause the furniture to move if not held on to).

3.If outdoors, get into an open area away from trees, buildings, walls, and power lines.

4.Stay away from windows, shelves, heavy objects, or anything that can fall.

5.Do not rush for exit. You may be struck by falling debris, stairways may be broken, and walkways may be jammed with people.

6.DO NOT use elevators, as the power may be off.

(b) After Earthquake-

1.Go to your predetermined outdoor meeting location. Check for injuries to others and provide assistance as needed.

2.Check for and extinguish small fires. Clean up any spilled flammable liquids immediately.

3. Shut off the main gas valve only if you suspect a gas leak. Return home only when it is declared safe.

4.Use the telephone only for emergencies. 911 systems will be overloaded after an earthquake. Keep the phone lines clear for emergency calls to get through.

5.Be prepared for aftershocks! Aftershocks can occur minutes, days, or months after an earthquake. Drop, Cover, and Hold on each time shaking occurs.

(iv) CPR Steps:

Use CPR when an adult is not breathing or when they are only gasping occasionally, and when they are not responding to questions or taps on the shoulder.

In children and infants, use CPR when they are not breathing normally and not responding.

Check that the area is safe, then perform the following basic CPR steps:

1.Call 911 or ask someone else to.

2.Lay the person on their back and open their airway.

3.Check for breathing. If they are not breathing, start CPR.

4.Perform 30 chest compressions.

5.Perform two rescue breaths.

6.Repeat until an ambulance or automated external defibrillator (AED) arrives.
